# Portfolio Reflection Addendum

At the beginning of this course, I viewed Artificial Intelligence as one of the most powerful tools available today. It felt like almost anything could be accomplished with a simple prompt. While I was familiar with some AI applications and had even worked with AI solutions during a previous internship, I did not fully understand how modern AI systems worked or the limitations they faced.

The midterm exam was a keypoint during the semester. By then, we had already completed projects involving AI and had studied several important topics, including Large Language Models and hybrid systems. During one of the exam discussions, I explained why an LLM can sound correct while still being wrong. I argued that LLMs are designed to generate responses based on patterns found in the data they were trained on. They predict the most probable next tokens based on those patterns, not by verifying whether the information provided to the user is actually correct. This was one of the first times I stopped looking at AI simply as a tool and started thinking more critically about how it produces its answers.

This realization connected directly with the essay and debate on the question, *"Is AI real intelligence that is artificial?"* Before the debate, I viewed intelligence as the ability to interpret information and use it in applicable situations. If a system could answer questions, solve problems, and interact naturally with humans, it seemed reasonable to consider it intelligent. However, researching the topic exposed me to different perspectives regarding consciousness, reasoning, understanding, and self-awareness. While modern AI systems can imitate many aspects of intelligent behavior, I now recognize that there is an important distinction between producing intelligent-looking responses and genuinely understanding information.

Throughout the semester, I learned that no single AI technique is capable of solving every problem. Different situations require different approaches, and understanding the strengths and limitations of each method is just as important as knowing how to use them. This broader perspective changed the way I think about Artificial Intelligence and helped me understand why the field continues to evolve through the combination of many different ideas and techniques.

In conclusion, this course significantly changed the way I think about Artificial Intelligence. Rather than viewing AI as a single technology, I now understand it as a broad field composed of many complementary approaches. More importantly, I learned that effective AI systems require not only powerful models but also careful design, validation, and ethical responsibility. This perspective will be valuable as I continue exploring Artificial Intelligence both academically and professionally.
