# Artemis II Mission and Intelligent Agents

## Summary

These sources were used to analyze the Artemis II mission through the perspective of intelligent agents. The materials provided information regarding spacecraft systems, communication networks, mission objectives, and the interaction between automated systems and human operators. Using concepts from *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach*, different components of the mission were classified as simple reflex, model-based, goal-based, utility-based, and human agents.

These readings helped connect intelligent agent theory to a real-world aerospace system. They reinforced concepts such as PEAS, rational agents, perception, decision-making, and human-in-the-loop systems. The assignment demonstrated how multiple agent architectures can work together within a complex engineering environment.

# Environmental Impact of Artificial Intelligence

## Summary

These articles examined the environmental costs associated with modern Artificial Intelligence systems, particularly Large Language Models. The readings discussed energy consumption, carbon emissions, water usage for data center cooling, and the growing computational requirements of training and deploying advanced AI models. The sources also highlighted concerns regarding sustainability and the unequal distribution of environmental impacts across different regions.

These materials connected directly to discussions on AI ethics and responsible AI development. They demonstrated that AI systems should not only be evaluated based on performance and capabilities, but also on their environmental and societal consequences. The readings reinforced the importance of considering ethical responsibilities when developing and deploying AI technologies.

# Python and NumPy Documentation

## Summary

These documentation sources were reviewed while developing the performance benchmarking project that compared pure Python implementations against NumPy implementations for vector and matrix multiplication. The Python documentation provided information regarding language features, loops, data structures, and execution behavior, while the NumPy documentation explained vectorized operations, array manipulation, and optimized numerical computations.

The documentation also provided insight into how NumPy performs large-scale numerical operations more efficiently than standard Python implementations by utilizing optimized compiled libraries and specialized data structures.

These resources supported the implementation of the first programming assignment and reinforced concepts related to computational efficiency, algorithmic complexity, and performance analysis. Although the course primarily focused on Artificial Intelligence, many AI applications rely heavily on numerical computation and matrix operations. Understanding the differences between pure Python and NumPy helped demonstrate why optimized numerical libraries are widely used in machine learning, data science, and AI systems.

The project also highlighted the importance of selecting appropriate tools and implementations when developing computationally intensive applications, a concept that is relevant across many areas of Artificial Intelligence.
